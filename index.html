About Hindi Font Conversion

Before Unicode became the standard for digital Hindi text, numerous legacy fonts like Krutidev, Shree Lipi, Chanakya, DevLys, and Shusha were widely used in India for Hindi typing, publishing, and government documentation. These fonts use non-standard character encodings that appear as garbled text on modern systems.

Our free online tools help you convert text between these legacy Hindi font encodings and Unicode (Mangal), which is the universal standard for Devanagari script. Whether you are working with old government documents, newspaper archives, or legacy publishing files, these converters make it easy to bring your Hindi text into the modern digital world.
